Project Tools and Tech

Raspberry Pi 4 Model B – chosen for its small size, low cost, strong 64-bit processor (which may be important with future developments), large ram, huge developer support and low power consumption. It also has wifi onboard and can be connected to a local area network via ethernet, if needed.

Pixy 2 - the standout camera for balancing reasonable cost with high frame rate and great, high-definition results in low light conditions.

Ubuntu – open source and stable distribution of Linux, with no cost, capable of being run on the ARM processor of the Raspberry Pi 4. The operating system has regular updates, which is useful for security and stability.

Python 3 – open source, and less complex, programming language which is a dependency for Tensorflow Lite, and can be used for scripting, including triggering imager capture and file handling.

Pip – package installer for Python, which assists in installing the required packages on Ubuntu.

Microsoft Lobe – currently in Beta, we look forward to its full release. This service significantly simplified the training the TensorFlow Lite model, with excellent accuracy in object recognition in testing. Currently a no-cost service from Microsoft.

Tensorflow Lite - the preferred Visual Recognition/Artificial Intelligence service for this device. Currently a free, cloud service, it is mature and well supported.

SQLite – an open source, no cost, lightweight database, well suited to the energy efficient and low resources of a RaspberryPi.
